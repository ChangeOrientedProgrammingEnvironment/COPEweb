For all Java-developers who use IntelliJ IDEA to work on personal, class or open-source projects! You have a chance to make valuable scientific contribution without spending any intellectual effort!

Group of researchers from Oregon State University, University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign and University of Texas at Austin conduct study of TDD practices used by professional Java-developers in their everyday life and work. Researchers need your help with beta-testing of plugin for IntelliJ IDEA IDE. Plugin has been developed by researchers to collect data about source code edits, runs of unit-tests and unit-test results performed in IntelliJ IDEA IDE. Plugin records information required for the study and sends data regularly to the server located at Oregon State University for further analysis. If you are Java-developer using IntelliJ IDEA and you expect to work on personal, class or open-source project in the near future, all you need to do is:

  1. Accept informed consent form.
  2. Proceed with installation instructions of COPE plugin for IntelliJ IDEA.
  3. Fill in short questionnaire after you have successfully installed COPE plugin.
  4. Proceed with Java-development.

If you encounter any errors, please report it using this form. You automatically become research participant once you install COPE plugin even if you do not get a chance to send error report. You can stop your participation in the study by uninstalling plugin (you can find uninstallation instructions here).

If you need to receive study report after the study is complete, you will need to:

  • Participate in the study by keeping COPE Plugin installed until the end date of data collection phase.
  • Provide your e-mail for the feedback as a part of questionnaire.

Personalized report will contain:

  • Detailed description of TDD practices you used over the course of data collection phase.
  • Personalized statistics and metrics, calculated as a result of data analysis phase.
  • Information about personal rating with regard to other study participants. Rating will be calculated based on established maturity criteria for TDD practices used by every study participant.
